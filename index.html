Know the Difference

Is Owner Operator the Right Fit?

Being an OO has real upside — but it's not for everyone. Here's an honest look at what you're signing up for.

✅ Owner Operator Upside

  • Higher gross earnings per mile — you keep a larger share of load revenue
  • Full control over your schedule and load selection
  • You own your equipment — build equity, not just a paycheck
  • Tax advantages available to self-employed contractors
  • Operate under GMH's authority — no need for your own MC number to get started
  • Build a business, not just a career

📌 What You're Responsible For

  • Fuel costs come out of your revenue (biggest variable expense)
  • Truck payments, maintenance, and repairs are on you
  • You carry your own insurance (bobtail, occupational accident, etc.)
  • No company-provided health insurance or paid time off
  • Income can vary more week to week than a company driver
  • Self-employment taxes — budget for quarterly estimates

👉 If you're on the fence — reach out to Matt directly. He'll give you a straight answer on whether OO or company driver is the better fit for your situation.

Qualifications

What We're Looking For

We partner with owner operators who run a professional operation. Here's what you'll need.

✅ Minimum Requirements

  • Valid CDL-A license
  • Your own Class 8 tractor (truck)
  • Minimum 2 years verifiable driving experience
  • Clean MVR (Motor Vehicle Record)
  • Ability to pass DOT physical and drug screen
  • Must be 23 years of age or older
  • Adequate bobtail and occupational accident insurance
  • No more than 2 moving violations in past 3 years

⭐ Preferred (But Not Required)

  • Your own trailer (or willing to use GMH drop equipment)
  • 3+ years OTR, regional, or owner operator experience
  • Experience with ELD (Electronic Logging Devices)
  • Reefer or flatbed experience a plus
  • Familiarity with Northeast / Mid-Atlantic freight lanes
  • Established DOT authority (or willing to operate under ours)
  • Strong safety record — we prioritize partners who do too
